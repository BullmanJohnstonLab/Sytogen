MyMotif

MyMotif allows users to define, import, or edit the restriction-modification (RM) target sequence motifs from a bacterial strain or collection of strains.

RM motifs are the short DNA sequences that restriction endonucleases (REase) recognize as foreign when they are inappropriately methylated or unmethylated; they therefore determine the exact positions in a genetic construct that can be altered during RM-silencing to improve transformation efficiency.

About MyMotif

MyMotif provides a standardized way to create or refine the RM motif set for a bacterial strain, whether or not full PacBio methylome data are available. SyToGen depends on having an accurate and complete list of RM motifs in a bacterium of interest, which can vary between species, between strains, and even within clonal lineages due to differences in RM system composition (including phase-variable RM systems). Without reliable motif information for a strain or collection of strains, RM-silencing cannot be performed effectively, thus it critical to know which RM motifs need to be addressed to improve transformation efficiency.

MyMotif therefore allows users to inspect, adjust, or supplement their SyToGen compatible motif list; whether to correct incomplete annotations, incorporate new experimentally derived motifs, merge motif collections from multiple strains of interest, or resolve ambiguities introduced by strain-to-strain variation (for example degenerate sequences or motif miscalls).

Additionally, in some cases certain motifs may not require sequence-based RM-silencing because alternative approaches are available. Certain specific motifs can be readily masked by in vitro methylation using matching commercially available methyltransferases or by passage through surrogate strains that introduce the appropriate methylation required to protect against RM activity (for instance, RM systems that recognize Gm6ATC modification can be readily bypassed using common Dam methylase E. coli strains). MyMotif will thus highlight to users when these alternative approaches are available and may be feasible, while still providing the accurate motif list required for downstream steps in the SyToGen workflow.

What does MyMotif do?

MyMotif collects and prepares the RM motif information required for downstream steps in the SyToGen workflow. It imports motif sets from PacBio methylome files, REBASE annotations, or manual user-provided lists, and allows users to curate or refine these motifs as needed. The module validates motif structure, resolves formatting inconsistencies, and standardizes the motif table so that it can be used reliably by MotifFinder and the core SyToGen module.
